During the calibration phase, RANSHIELD monitors baseline operations to establish statistical bounds ($H \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$) for healthy executable workflows.

Mathematical Foundations

Formula (5): Adaptive Thresholding

$$\tau_{enc} = \mu_H + 2.5 \cdot \sigma_H$$

To avoid false positives from common applications that write high-entropy content (like compiling source files, compressed document saves, or image compression), RANSHIELD assigns distinct classes upon calibration finalization:

  • Low Entropy Workloads (Office/Text): Processes executing typical plain text or simple structured writes are restricted to tighter thresholds ($\tau_{enc} = 5.2$ - $6.5$).
  • Medium Workloads (Media/Binary): Standard productivity utilities, image processors, and browsers are assigned standard thresholds ($\tau_{enc} = 6.5$ - $7.2$).
  • High Entropy Workloads (Compilers/Dev): Processes doing native zip, encrypting, or compiling operations are allocated maximum bounds ($\tau_{enc} = 7.2$ - $7.8$) to minimize false flags.
